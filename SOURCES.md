## Japanese Art & Ukiyo-e

### Primary Artwork

- **Utagawa Kuniyoshi** (歌川国芳). *Sōma no furudairi* (相馬の古内裏, "The Old Palace at Sōma"), also known as "Takiyasha the Witch and the Skeleton Spectre", 1844. Woodblock print triptych.
  - Most famous depiction of giant skeleton in Japanese art
  - Depicts Princess Takiyasha summoning skeleton spectre
  - Defines visual representation of gashadokuro

## Historical Sources

### Princess Takiyasha Legend

- *Taiheiki* (太平記) — Medieval Japanese epic chronicling wars and rebellions
- *Masakadoki* (将門記) — Chronicles of Taira no Masakado's rebellion (10th century)
- Various Edo period adaptations and retellings

### Famine Records

- Historical records of Japanese famines:
  - Tenmei Famine (1782-1788)
  - Tenpō Famine (1833-1837)
  - Various regional famines throughout history
